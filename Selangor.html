Selangor

Selangor became part of the Federation of Malaysia in 1963 and its capital Kuala Lumpur became the administration centre in trade, commerce and banking. In 1974, Selangor handed Kuala Lumpur to the federal government and Shah Alam became the new capital.

Places to visit in Selangor

Batu Caves

A limestone outcrop located just north of Kuala Lumpur, Batu Caves has three main caves featuring temples and Hindu shrines. Its main attraction is the large statue of the Hindu God at the entrance, besides a steep 272 climb up its steps to finally view the stunning skyline of the city centre.

Image by Elizabeth Lvis, https://www.travelawaits.com/2484474/batu-caves-malaysia/.

Zoo Negara

The Zoo Negara (Natonal Zoo) is a zoo in Malaysia located on 110 acres (45 ha) of land in Ulu Klang, Gombak District, Selangor, Malaysia. It was officially opened on November 14, 1963 by the country's first prime minister Tunku Abdul Rahman. The zoo is managed by a non-governmental organization known as the Malaysian Zoological Society and is home to 5,137 animals of 476 different species.

Image by Mahani Ishak, https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2020/11/756094/zoo-negara-tidak-boleh-bergantung-kepada-kerajaan.

Firefly Tour

Escape the hustle and bustle of Kuala Lumpur and head over the calmer and quieter hamlet town of Kuala Selangor. Your day starts with a visit to Kota Melawati, also known as Fort Altingsburg, built to defend the city against invasions all the way back in the 18th century. If you are lucky, catch a glimpse of the rare silvered leaf monkeys. The highlight of the trip is a boat trip along the banks of Kampung Kuantan where you can watch a stunning fireflies show and reward yourself with a delicious seafood dinner after the boat trip.

Image by Asni Tours and Travels.

City to visit in Selangor

Shah Alam

Shah Alam is a city and the state capital of Selangor, Malaysia and situated within the Petaling District and a small portion of the neighbouring Klang District. Shah Alam replaced Kuala Lumpur as the capital city of the state of Selangor in 1978 due to Kuala Lumpur's incorporation into a Federal Territory in 1974. Shah Alam was the first planned city in Malaysia after independence from Britain in 1957.
