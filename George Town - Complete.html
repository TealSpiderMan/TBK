George Town

History

George Town is the first city in Malaysia. The City centre has been inscribe as a UNESCO World Heritage Site singe 2008. Today, the city, well known for its cultural diversity, colonial-era architecture and street food, is a booming tourist destination and still serves as the financial centre of northern Malaysia.

Some Fun Facts about George Town

George Town is renowned for its vibrant street art scene. You can find colourful murals, installations and scultures all over the city. Furthermore, George Town is also home to the Oldest Anglican Church and also the Largest Buddhist Temple in Southeast Asia, truly a Melting Pot of Cultures.

Little India

Picture by simonlong - https://www.tripsavvy.com/top-things-to-do-in-george-town-penang-4687174

The Little India is a main business district in Georgetown. Various Indian related businesses can be found here. There are many Indian fashion stores which sell mostly saree, silk and cotton. While you walk by the shops, and Bollywood music setting the festive mood, Penang's famous Banana Leaf Rice is one to be prioritised while you are here. The Oldest Hindu Temple, the colourful Sri Mahamariamman Temple is also located in Little India.​

Fort Cornwallis

Picture by Pangalau - retrieved from Wikipedia

Don't get bamboozled by the word Corn, Fort Cornwallis, named after Lieutenant-General The 2nd Earl Cornwallis, is the largest standing fort in Malaysia. While it has never engaged in actual combat during its operation, it is decorated with old cannons, it is said that the main purpose of the fort is to protect Penang from pirates and Kedah.

George Town: Delicacies

Well, it won't be Penang without good Penang food. Here are some delicacies you can check out while in George Town:

Penang Famous Samosa

A Samosa is a South Asian pastry with either savoury or sweet filling. In Penang Famous Samosa, (ps. everything in Penang has "famous" in front of it, don't ask me why) they serve Chicken Samosa (Curry Chicken, crispy and aromatic), Egg Samosa (hard-boiled egg), Masala Vadai (Our personal favourite with many spices), Sweet Cake (Sweet and Milky) and Jelebi (Deep Fried Donut).

Image by Abby Choi, Google Reviews.

Siam Road Charcoal Char Kuey Teow

Siam Road Char Koay Teow is without a doubt the most famous Char Koay Teow place in Penang. Not only they sit above the rest in popularity but also taste above average as well. The 81-year-old uncle Tan, still stands firmly, frying that oily goodness for people to savour. Some even call him the King of Noodles and the oldest char kway teow legend in Malaysia. If you are in George Town, please do not hesitate to visit his gerai.

Image by synthiatjipto @Instagram.

Nasi Kandar Line Clear

Located in an Alleyway, but the fragrant smell of Indian spices and curry might lead some visitors astray. Classified a true Malaysian street food, Nasi Kandar Line Clear is a must have while you are in George Town. Known for their Nasi Kuah Campur and fish head curry, you might also want to try their prawns, chicken, sotong, beef and vegetables. Or we would suggest to mix everything into one delicious goodness! However, the queue is usually quite long, so don't keep your stomach too empty (ps. eat something small first like a samosa).

Image by TheHive.Asia.

Kebaya Restaurant

This fancy restaurant with thick drapery, pendant chandeliers, and colorful tiled floors hints at the high prices you’ll be paying for your meal. But if you’re willing to splurge, this Nyonya fine dining is the way to do it. Start your meal with the Pai Tee, a shy platter of shredded vegetables and sweet prawns in crispy “cups,” before moving on to the Lor Ark (duck confit with spices) and the Crispy Nyonya Tempura (deep fried vegetables in delicate batter).
